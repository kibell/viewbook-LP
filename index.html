Residence Life

And you belong at SNHU

We want you to feel right at home. That’s why we offer a variety of residence halls, suites and apartments to fit your needs. If you need a multi-purpose classroom to collaborate with your peers or a fitness center to work on your gains, we’ve got you.

School of Arts, Sciences and Education

Let’s bring learning to life

At SNHU, you could learn how to teach the next generation of learners or how to spin a PR mishap into a viral moment. You could also spend your days looking through a microscope or wandering the 24 acres of trails in our on-campus arboretum.

School of Business

Choose your own venture

We know the best way to learn is by experiencing it for yourself. That’s why our students are busy conducting business simulations, interning for local sports teams and starting their own businesses. Plus, with our group case studies and on-campus competitions, you can graduate with the experience employers want.

School of Engineering, Technology and Aeronautics

Hands on. Limits off.

From writing computer code to 3D printing, if you can dream it, you can build it. You’ll even work in multi-major groups to solve real-world problems in spaces like the robotics lab, wood shop and drone arena. Here, you’re only limited to your imagination. And with the help of faculty mentors and collaborative peers, the world is yours to create.

Athletics

Let’s catch a game

You might be an athlete playing on one of our 19 NCAA Division II teams. Maybe you’re planning to join a varsity, intramural or club team. Whether you’re ready to win a championship or cheer from the stands with 1,500 of your friends, we’re here to get loud!

Esports

You’ve gotta see this

Join our varsity esports team and take on students across the country in League of Legends, Overwatch, Rocket League, Super Smash Bros. or Valorant. Who knows? Maybe you’ll help lead us to nationals… again. PS – if competing on a varsity team isn’t your style, our state-of-the-art arena is still open for you.

Manchester

Where are we meeting?

Living like a local is more than knowing the best food spots in town (and there’s a lot of ‘em). It’s cheering on the Fisher Cats with your best friends. It’s taking a watercolor class at the Currier Museum of Art. It’s catching a performance at the Palace Theatre. But if you’re looking to head out of town, you can also find mountains, beaches and Boston only an hour away.
